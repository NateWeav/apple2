iTunes Content Dispute

Welcome

If you believe that content available on the iTunes Store or Apple Music, or on Apple Podcasts, is in violation of your intellectual property rights, you can use this form to submit a claim to the iTunes Store Legal Team. If you are looking for the form to submit a claim for an app available on the App Store, please visit here. If you are looking for the form to submit a claim for a ringtone, please visit here.

Content available on the iTunes Store or Apple Music are provided to iTunes by third party content providers. Once you have submitted your dispute using this form, we will contact the provider of the disputed content regarding your claim. In most cases, we will ask that the provider work with you directly to resolve the issue.

For podcasts, iTunes simply provides a directory of user-submitted podcasts that are available for download from third parties’ servers. Please make sure that your submitted dispute complies with the notice requirements of the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA), which this form is designed to facilitate.

Select Disputed Content

The most accurate way to let us know exactly what content you are disputing is to provide a link from the iTunes Store. If you do not have a link, you can choose the search tab below to find the content at issue. If you choose to use the search option, please note that the country name drop-down menu will allow you to search iTunes storefronts throughout the world. It is not necessary to select the same content in multiple countries. On the next page, you will have the ability to specify the country(ies) where you are disputing rights.
