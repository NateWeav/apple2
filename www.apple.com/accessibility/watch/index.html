A watch that considers all the different ways you move.

Apple Watch tracks your daily activity, measures workouts, and encourages healthy routines. And because it’s built to be accessible, you can get accurate fitness tracking however you move, feel a gentle tap when it’s time for a meeting, or hear a text message read aloud.

Walkie-Talkie Get in touch with just a tap.

Walkie-Talkie is an entirely new way to quickly and easily communicate. Right from your wrist, you can instantly start a conversation with anyone who has a compatible Apple Watch. And a gentle tap or sound lets you know when you’re in touch.

Activity and Workout Apps Reach your fitness goals. No matter how you get there.

We’ve optimized the Activity app for wheelchair users, taking into account different pushing techniques for varying speeds and terrain. There are also two wheelchair-specific workouts and a Time to Roll notification.

Explore all features for physical and motor skills

VoiceOver You don’t need to see your Apple Watch to use your Apple Watch.

VoiceOver is a gesture-based screen reader that tells you exactly what’s happening on your Apple Watch, and helps you navigate it, without the need to see the screen.

Explore all features for blind and low vision

Taptic Engine Get a feeling for what’s going on.

Beeps and chimes aren’t the only way to know you’ve received a notification. The Taptic Engine can give you a gentle tap. So you’ll never miss an upcoming turn, new message, or last-minute party invitation.

Explore all features for deaf and hard of hearing

Enable accessibility features on Apple Watch.

  • On Apple Watch

    1. Open Settings.
    2. Select General, then Accessibility.
    3. Tap to turn on the features you want to use.

  • On iPhone

    1. Open Watch.
    2. In the My Watch tab, select General, then Accessibility.
    3. Tap to turn on the features you want to use.
